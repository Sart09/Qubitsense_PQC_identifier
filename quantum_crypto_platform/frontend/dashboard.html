Quantum Threat Explanation

This asset depends on classical cryptographic primitives that are mathematically vulnerable to attacks from cryptographically relevant quantum computers (CRQC). Specifically, primitives relying on integer factorization (e.g., RSA) or discrete logarithms (e.g., ECC, Diffie-Hellman) can be broken exponentially faster using Shor's algorithm.

Adversaries can perform a "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" (HNDL) attack by capturing these encrypted communications today, retaining them in storage, and subsequently decrypting the traffic once quantum computing technology matures.
